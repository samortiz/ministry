CHAPTER ONE

THE ASPIRATION OF THE LORD’S DESIRE— THE ONENESS OF HIS BELIEVERS

Scripture Reading: John 17:2, 6, 11b, 14-23; Matt. 18:19; Acts 1:14; 1 Cor. 1:10; Phil. 2:2b; Eph. 4:4-6; 1 Cor. 4:17b; 7:17b; 11:16; 14:34a; Rev. 2:7a, 11a, 17a, 29; 3:6, 13, 22; 1:20

OUTLINE

  1. The aspiration of the Lord’s desire becoming His specific prayer:
    1. Before going to the cross.
    2. Praying to the Father.
    3. Earnestly desiring that all His believers be one.
      1. One in the Father’s name and life—John 17:2, 6, 11b.
        1. The Father being the source, and the Father’s name indicating the Father Himself (the person) as the source.
        2. The Father’s life with His nature as the element of oneness in the person of the Father.
      2. One in the reality of the sanctifying word—John 17:14-21.
        1. The word of God having God Himself as reality.
        2. God’s word of reality sanctifying us from the mixed world unto Himself.
      3. One in the glory that expresses divinity—John 17:22-23.
        1. The Father’s glory indicating the divine life with the divine nature, expressing divinity in splendor.
        2. The splendid expression of divinity delivers us from ourselves.
  2. The practice of oneness:
    1. The one accord and the oneness in mind, in the church life—Matt. 18:19; Acts 1:14.
    2. Speaking the same thing, being attuned in the same mind and in the same opinion—1 Cor. 1:10.
    3. Thinking the same thing, having the same love, being joined in soul—Phil. 2:2b.
    4. Based on the attribute of the oneness of the church—Eph. 4:4-6.
    5. According to the apostles’ teaching—1 Cor. 4:17b; 7:17b; 11:16; 14:34a.
    6. According to the same words that the Spirit spoke to the churches—Rev. 2:7a, 11a, 17a, 29; 3:6, 13, 22.
    7. Indicating that the seven churches as the seven golden lampstands are completely identical—Rev. 1:20.
