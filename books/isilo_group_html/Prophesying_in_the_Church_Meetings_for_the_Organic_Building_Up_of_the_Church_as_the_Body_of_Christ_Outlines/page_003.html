OUTLINE TWO

PROPHESYING
IN THE CHURCH MEETINGS
FOR THE ORGANIC BUILDING UP
OF THE CHURCH AS THE BODY OF CHRIST

Scripture Reading: Luke 10:1-6; Rom. 15:16; 1 Pet. 2:5; John 21:15; 1 Thes. 2:7; Eph. 4:11-12; Heb. 10:24-25; 1 Cor. 14:3-5, 26

  1. The organic way to practice the Lord’s present recovery for the organic building up of the Body of Christ:
    1. The organic preaching of the gospel—saving sinners by contacting them where they are:
      1. God’s pattern in the Old Testament:
        1. Coming to find and visit fallen man (Gen. 3:8-9), preaching the gospel to fallen man (3:15), and ministering salvation to the condemned man (3:21).
        2. Coming down to visit Israel to deliver them out of the tyranny of Pharaoh in Egypt— Exo. 3:8.
      2. The example of the Lord Jesus:
        1. Coming down from heaven to save sinners—1 Tim. 1:15.
        2. Visiting Zaccheus with His dynamic salvation—Luke 19:1-10.
        3. Visiting the Samaritan woman to bring her the living water—John 4:3-15.
        4. Journeying from city to city and village to village with the twelve to proclaim and bring the good news of the kingdom of God— Luke 8:1.
      3. The Lord’s sending the disciples:
        1. Sending the twelve to go to the cities and villages to visit the lost sheep of the house of Israel and bring them peace (Matt. 10:5-8, 11-13).
        2. Sending the seventy to go to every city and place to seek the sons of peace (Luke 10:1-6).
        3. Charging His disciples to go into all the world and preach the gospel to all the creation (Mark 16:15).
        4. Charging His disciples to go and disciple all the nations by baptizing them into the Triune God (Matt. 28:19).
      4. Going out to visit people being the most effective way to preach the gospel.
      5. The meaning of the organic preaching of the gospel:
        1. To impart Christ into the repentant and believing sinners (Gal. 1:16).
        2. To minister Christ in His riches to people (Eph. 3:8).
        3. To beget sinners as children of God that they may become the members of the organic Body of Christ—1 Cor. 4:15; Rom. 12:5.
        4. To baptize the believing sinners into the Triune God—Matt 28:19.
        5. To bear fruit as an issue of the genuine enjoyment of Christ—John 15:5, 16a.
      6. The organic labor of preaching the gospel:
        1. Making the New Testament priesthood of the gospel a part of our daily life by saving sinners and offering them to God as spiritual sacrifices—Rom. 15:16; 1 Pet. 2:5.
        2. Laboring in the gospel habitually, regularly, and constantly—2 Tim. 4:2; 1 Cor. 9:22-23.
    2. The organic nourishing and cherishing of the new believers in their homes:
      1. Meeting with them regularly in their homes until they become established in the faith— Acts 5:42.
      2. Feeding them as the Lord’s lambs—John 21:15:
        1. Bringing them into the practice of exercising their spirit and calling on the Lord— Eph. 6:18a; Rom. 10:12b.
        2. Teaching them the basic truths of God’s redemption.
      3. Being a nursing mother to nourish and cherish them—1 Thes. 2:7:
        1. Taking care of their spiritual need and their need in practical affairs, sicknesses, and afflictions.
        2. Praying for them.
      4. Bringing them up and leading them to present themselves to God as living sacrifices—Rom. 12:1.
    3. The organic perfecting of the saints in the small group meetings:
      1. Participating in the fellowship, interceding, and mutual care with the saints—Acts 2:42, 46; 12:5, 12.
      2. Inciting one another to love and good works, exhorting one another—Heb. 10:24-25.
      3. Learning and teaching the truth and the experience of life mutually—Acts 2:42.
      4. Preaching the gospel—Acts 5:42; 2:46-47.
      5. Entreating and consoling the believers as a father—1 Thes. 2:11; Acts 20:20, 31.
      6. Leading the believers to grow into Christ, the Head, in all things, to a full-grown man, unto the work of ministry—the organic work for the building up of the Body of Christ—Eph. 4:11-16; Col. 1:28-29.
      7. Carrying out the practical church life.
    4. The organic prophesying in the larger meetings of mutuality for the building up of the church as the organic Body of Christ:
      1. All prophesying mutually—1 Cor. 14:24, 26.
      2. Speaking building up, encouragement, and consolation—1 Cor. 14:3.
      3. Convicting and judging people, exposing the secrets of people’s hearts—1 Cor. 14:24-25.
      4. The characteristics of the church meetings of mutuality according to the God-ordained way of “each one has” (1 Cor. 14:26):
        1. A “one another” meeting, a “round-table” meeting:
          1. 1) Speaking to one another—Eph. 5:19.
          2. 2) Teaching and admonishing one another— Col. 3:16.
          3. 3) Considering one another and exhorting one another—Heb. 10:24-25.
          4. 4) Listening to one another—1 Thes. 5:20.
        2. Making the believers living.
        3. Developing the organic ability and function of the living members of Christ—Eph. 4:16.
        4. Fitting the living believers’ taste.
        5. Renouncing “the customs of the nations”— 2 Kings 17:8 (NASB).
        6. Building up the church as the organic Body of Christ—1 Cor. 14:4-5, 26.
