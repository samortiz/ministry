THE TEN GREAT CRITICAL “ONES” FOR THE BUILDING UP OF THE BODY OF CHRIST

MESSAGE TWO

ONE CHRIST-THE UNSEARCHABLE ONE

(1)

OUTLINE

  1. Christ is the eternal and only begotten Son of God, the Second of the Divine Trinity-John 1:1-2, 18; Matt. 28:19.
  2. He is self-existing, ever-existing, and coinhering with the Father and the Spirit in eternity.
  3. He is, with the Father and the Spirit, the only God-John 1:1; Rom. 9:5; Heb. 1:8.
  4. He is the Creator (Heb. 1:10), and all things were created through Him (John 1:3; Col. 1:16a), cohere in Him (Col. 1:17), and will be an inheritance unto Him as the Heir (Col. 1:16b; Heb. 1:2; Rom. 11:34-36).
  5. In time He became a man through His incarnation, a God-man having both divinity and humanity as His nature-John 1:1, 14; Heb. 2:14.
  6. He is the Firstborn of all creation-Col. 1:15b.
  7. He is the effulgence of God’s glory and the impress of God’s substance-Heb. 1:3a.
  8. He is the embodiment of God-Col. 2:9.
  9. He is the image of the invisible God-Col. 1:15a.
  10. He lived in His humanity on the earth to express God for thirty-three and a half years-He is God manifested in the flesh-1 Tim. 3:16.
  11. He is the faithful Witness of God-Rev. 1:5.
  12. He is the tabernacle of God as God’s movable dwelling place among men-John 1:14.
  13. He is the temple of God as God’s established dwelling place on earth-John 2:19-21.
  14. He is the light of the world-John 8:12.
  15. He died an all-inclusive death through which He crucified Satan-the old serpent (John 3:14; Heb. 2:14)-sin (Rom. 8:3; John 1:29), the world (John 12:31), the fallen man with his flesh (Rom. 6:6; Gal. 2:20), the old creation (represented by the old fallen man), and the law with its ordinances (Rom. 10:4; Col. 2:14; Eph. 2:15) for the accomplishment of God’s eternal redemption (Heb. 9:12), and released His divine life from the shell of His humanity to be dispensed into His believers (John 12:24; 3:15).
  16. He resurrected from among the dead (Matt. 28:6-7; 1 Cor. 15:4b), and through His resurrection He in His humanity was designated to be the firstborn Son of God (Rom. 1:4; 8:29; Acts 13:33), became the life-giving Spirit (1 Cor. 15:45), and regenerated with His resurrection life all the people chosen by God before the foundation of the world (1 Pet. 1:2-3; Eph. 1:4).
  17. He ascended to the heavens, and in His ascension He was crowned and enthroned to be:
    1. The Lord of all men and all things to rule over the whole universe-Acts 10:36.
    2. Christ-the Anointed of God-to accomplish God’s economy-Acts 2:36.
    3. The Ruler of the kings of the earth (Rev. 1:5) to arrange the situations of the world and the environment of God’s chosen people that they may be saved by Him as
    4. The Savior who will be received by all His believers as their everything-Acts 5:31.
    5. The Head of the church to take care of the church as His Body-Eph. 1:22-23; 5:29-30.
    6. The Paraclete in heaven before God to take care of all the cases and needs of His members (1 John 2:1), and the One who is realized by His Spirit as the Paraclete in His members (John 14:16-20).
    7. The heavenly Minister to minister heaven into His believers to make them the heavenly citizens-Heb. 8:2; Phil. 3:20.
    8. The great High Priest in the heavens to take care of His believers’ needs, intercede for them, and save them to the uttermost-Heb. 4:14-15; 7:25-27.
    9. The Mediator of the new covenant, bequeathed by Him to His believers through His death, to execute it-Heb. 8:6; 9:15.
    10. The surety of the better covenant to be its guarantee-Heb. 7:22.
