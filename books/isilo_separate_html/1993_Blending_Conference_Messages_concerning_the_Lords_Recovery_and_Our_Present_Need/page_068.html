MESSAGE SIX

THE LIFE-GIVING SPIRIT

Scripture Reading: Gen. 1:2; Judg. 6:34; Luke 1:15, 35; Matt. 1:20; John 7:39; 1 Cor. 15:45b; 2 Cor. 3:17-18; Rom. 8:2; Acts 1:8; Luke 24:49; 2 Kings 2:9, 13-15; Exo. 30:23-29; 2 Cor. 1:21; 1 John 2:20, 27; Acts 16:7; Rom. 8:9; Phil. 1:19b-21a; Rev. 1:4; 4:5; 5:6; 22:17

OUTLINE

  1. He was the Spirit of God in God's creation—Gen. 1:2.
  2. He was the Spirit of Jehovah in God's relationship with man—Judg. 6:34.
  3. He was the Holy Spirit in the conceptions of John the Baptist and the Lord Jesus in order to sanctify humanity for Christ's incarnation—Luke 1:15, 35; Matt. 1:20.
  4. The Spirit was not yet before Christ's resurrection, in which He was glorified—John 7:39.
  5. The last Adam became the life-giving Spirit as the pneumatic Christ in His resurrection—1 Cor. 15:45b.
  6. The Lord Spirit for transforming the believers into the glorious image of Christ—2 Cor. 3:17-18.
  7. The Spirit of life, the essential Spirit of God—Rom. 8:2.
  8. The Spirit of power, the economical Spirit of God, for the believers to put on as their uniform typified by the mantle of Elijah—Acts 1:8; Luke 24:49; 2 Kings 2:9, 13-15.
  9. As the compound Spirit, He was compounded with Christ's divinity, humanity, death with its effectiveness, and resurrection with its power to be the anointing ointment—Exo. 30:23-31.
  10. The anointing Spirit—2 Cor. 1:21; 1 John 2:20, 27.
  11. The Spirit of Jesus for the human life of Jesus, especially in His sufferings—Acts 16:7.
  12. The Spirit of Christ for Christ in resurrection in His divinity and His uplifted humanity—Rom. 8:9.
  13. The Spirit of Jesus Christ for His bountiful supply in His death and resurrection for the believers to live Christ and to magnify Christ—Phil. 1:19b-21a.
  14. The seven Spirits, the sevenfold intensified Spirit, for the overcomers in the degradation of the church—Rev. 1:4; 4:5; 5:6.
  15. Eventually, as the consummated Spirit, He becomes the Spirit to be the reality and realization of the pneumatic Christ and the consummation of the processed and consummated Triune God to be one with the bride of the Lamb (Christ) to proclaim the coming of Christ—Rev. 22:17.
