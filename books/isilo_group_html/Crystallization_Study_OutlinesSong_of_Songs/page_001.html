Crystallization-Study Outlines
Song of Songs

Message One

Pursuing Christ for Satisfaction

Scripture Reading: S.S. 1:2-4a

  1. Attracted by Christ’s love—1:2:
    1. Christ’s love is more cheering than wine.
    2. Nothing is so cheering as Christ’s love.
    3. His lover yearns for a direct kiss by Him with His mouth in His unrivaled love.
  2. Charmed by Christ’s name—1:3:
    1. Christ in His resurrection became the compounded Spirit (Exo. 30:23-30), who is compounded with the olive oil, signifying the Spirit of the Triune God, and with:
      1. Myrrh, signifying Christ’s sweet death.
      2. Cinnamon, signifying the sweet effectiveness of Christ’s death.
      3. Calamus, signifying Christ’s life-dispensing resurrection.
      4. Cassia, signifying the power of Christ’s resurrection.
    2. The name of such a consummated, all-inclusive, and life-giving Spirit sanctifies all the people and the things related to God’s move.
    3. Such a name is fragrant and more charming than everything else.
  3. Captivated by Christ’s person—1:4a:
    1. He has drawn and captivated millions of His lovers to pursue after Him and is still doing the same today.
    2. Therefore, all His lovers would run after Him for their satisfaction.
