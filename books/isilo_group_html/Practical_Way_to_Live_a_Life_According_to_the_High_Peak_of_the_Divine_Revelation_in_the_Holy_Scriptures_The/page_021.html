CHAPTER FIVE

TO BE CONFORMED
TO THE IMAGE OF GOD'S SON
THAT HE MIGHT BE THE FIRSTBORN AMONG MANY BROTHERS

Scripture Reading: Rom. 8:29

OUTLINE

  1. The image of God's Son:
    1. The image of the Lord Jesus Christ—Rom. 1:3-4:
      1. The Lord Jesus Christ being God and man.
      2. His image is the image of One who is both God and man.
      3. Having lived a life of man for the expression of God:
        1. A man whose living is not by his human life but by God's divine life.
        2. God's divine attributes becoming his human virtues.
    2. Such an image being the image of the first God-man in the universe, who is Christ.
  2. The predestination of God:
    1. The believers in Christ, who have been called and justified by God, being predestinated to be conformed to the image of the first God-man, Jesus Christ—Rom. 8:29-30.
    2. They becoming the many God-men as the many brothers (the mass reproduction) of the first God-man (the prototype).
    3. Thus, Jesus Christ as the only begotten Son of God (John 1:18) becoming the firstborn Son of God—Rom. 8:29-30:
      1. Christ, as the Son of God, putting humanity upon Himself in His incarnation.
      2. Although He being the Son of God possessing divinity, His human part not being divine, not being the Son of God.
      3. Christ's human part, as the seed of David, being designated, uplifted into His divinity by the Spirit of holiness (the essence of His divinity) in resurrection to become a part of His divine sonship.
      4. Thus, as the only begotten Son of God without humanity, Christ becoming the firstborn Son of God with both divinity and humanity.
      5. All the God-men, the believers in Christ, needing to be conformed to the image of such a firstborn Son of God, living a life crucified in the human life to live by the divine life that they may be the real God-men to be the expression of God in humanity with the divine attributes lived out from humanity, to be the reality of the church as the organic Body of Christ, consummating in the New Jerusalem, as revealed in details from the following chapters of Romans to the end of Revelation, for the carrying out of the eternal economy of God.
