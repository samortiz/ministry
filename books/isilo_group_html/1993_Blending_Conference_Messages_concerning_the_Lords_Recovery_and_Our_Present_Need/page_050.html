MESSAGE FIVE

THE TESTIMONY OF JESUS

Scripture Reading: Rev. 1:2, 9; 12:17; 19:10; 20:4b

OUTLINE

  1. The testimony of Jesus is the spirit—the reality, the substance, the disposition, and the characteristic—of the prophecy in the book of Revelation—Rev. 19:10b.
  2. The testimony of Jesus bears two denotations:
    1. A subjective denotation—the testimony by Jesus concerning God—Rev. 1:2; 22:16, 20a:
      1. As the true and faithful Witness of God (Rev. 1:5a; 3:14b), He bears testimony concerning God in what He is and in what He did and what He would do—John 5:19; 6:38; 7:16, 18.
      2. As the embodiment of God (Col. 2:9) to express God, He manifests God's attributes in His human virtues—1 Tim. 3:16.
    2. An objective denotation—the testimony by the believers with the church concerning Christ—Rev. 1:9; 12:17; 19:10a, b; 20:4.
  3. The testimony of the believers with the church by their living:
    1. Concerning Jesus' person—God and man—Phil. 2:6-7.
    2. Concerning Jesus' life—two lives living together: the divine life as the inward reality and the human life as the outward expression—1 Tim. 3:16.
    3. Concerning Jesus' living:
      1. Living a crucified life in resurrection—Phil. 2:8; Acts 2:23-24.
      2. With the divine attributes expressed in the human virtues—Acts 10:38.
    4. Concerning Jesus' work:
      1. Through His redeeming and life-releasing death—John 19:34; 12:24.
      2. Through His life-imparting and church-producing resurrection—1 Cor. 15:45; 1 Pet. 1:3.
      3. Through His ascension:
        1. To be made Lord, Christ, Ruler, and Savior—Acts 2:36; 5:31.
        2. To carry out His heavenly, church-building ministry—Heb. 8:1-2; Eph. 2:22.
      4. Through His second coming:
        1. To establish the eternal kingdom of God—Dan. 7:13-14; Rev. 11:15.
        2. To reign in the millennial kingdom—Rev. 20:4, 6.
  4. As the God-redeemed-regenerated-transformed-glorified people, signified by the following signs:
    1. The golden lampstands—the churches—Rev. 1:12-13, 20:
      1. Expressing the processed Triune God:
        1. In the Father's substance—the gold as the substance of the stands.
        2. In the Son's form—the embodied form of Christ as the shape of the stands.
        3. In the Spirit's expression—the bright expression by the shining of the lamps.
      2. With Christ in His humanity as the High Priest in the midst of the lampstands to trim them.
    2. The overcomers within the churches—Rev. 2:7, 11, 17, 26; 3:5, 12, 21:
      1. Overcoming Judaism, Catholicism, Protestantism, and Brethrenism—Rev. 2:9, 20; 3:1-2, 15-17.
      2. Overcoming the leaving of the first love toward Christ, worldliness, deadness, and lukewarmness—Rev. 2:4, 13-15; 3:1-2, 15-17.
    3. The man-child born of the universal woman—the universal church—Rev. 12:1-2, 5:
      1. Overcoming the great dragon, the ancient serpent, the devil, Satan, the adversary of God and the accuser of God's children—Rev. 12:7-10.
      2. By the blood of the Lamb, by the word of their testimony, and by their loving not their soul-life even unto death—Rev. 12:11.
    4. The firstfruits to God and to the Lamb—Rev. 14:1-5:
      1. Following Christ as the Lamb wherever He may go.
      2. Standing on Mount Zion (signifying surpassing all) with Christ as the Lamb.
    5. The late overcomers—the rest of the seed of the universal woman—Rev. 12:17:
      1. Overcoming Antichrist, his image, and the number of his name—666—in the great tribulation—Rev. 15:2; 13:12-18; 14:9-11.
      2. Standing on (signifying overcoming) the glassy sea—the lake of fire.
    6. The bride of the Lamb as the aggregate of all the overcomers—Rev. 19:7-9:
      1. Clothed in the overcoming and surpassing righteousness (Matt. 5:20) of the saints.
      2. To be the army with Christ to defeat and destroy Antichrist and his army at Armageddon—Rev. 17:14; 19:14, 19-21; 16:14, 16.
    7. The New Jerusalem, the tabernacle of God, the wife of the Lamb, as the ultimate consummation of all the matured saints—Rev. 21:1-3:
      1. To be the eternal manifestation and expression and the total increase of the processed and consummated Triune God in His mingling with all His redeemed, regenerated, transformed, and glorified saints.
      2. To be a triumphant show to all the universe, especially to His adversary Satan, of His victory in His infinite grace and glory through His redeemed in eternity (Eph. 2:7; 3:10). This is the ultimate and final testimony of Jesus! Hallelujah!
