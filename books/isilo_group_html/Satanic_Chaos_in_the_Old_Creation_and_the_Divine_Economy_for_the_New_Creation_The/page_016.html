2. Israel Entering into the Good Land

In Joshua 3 and 4 Israel entered into the good land. That was something of God's economy.

3. Israel Setting Up the Tabernacle
of the Ark in Shiloh

Another aspect of the divine economy was Israel's setting up the tabernacle of the ark in Shiloh (Josh. 18:1).

B. The Satanic Chaos with the Priests

Again the satanic chaos followed the divine economy.

1. The Priests among Israel Being Degraded

The priests among Israel became degraded and corrupt (1 Sam. 2:12-17, 22-34).

2. The Ark Being Captured by Israel's Enemy

In this chaotic situation, the ark, which was used in a superstitious way, was captured by Israel's enemy (1 Sam. 4:1—5:2). The priests were killed, and the ark was captured.

C. The Divine Economy
with David, Solomon, and the Positive Prophets

1. God Raising Up David
as a Man after His Own Heart to Do His Will

In the divine economy, God raised up David as a man after His own heart to do His will (1 Sam. 13:14).

2. David Bringing Back the Ark of God

David brought back the ark of God (2 Sam. 6:1-19).

3. David Desiring to Build the Temple for God
and Preparing the Material
and the Site for the Building of God's Temple

David desired to build the temple for God and he prepared the material and the site for the building of God's temple (2 Sam. 7:2; 1 Chron. 22:1-19; 29:1-19; 2 Chron. 3:1).

4. Solomon Building the Temple for God
and Bringing the Ark into the Temple

Solomon built the temple for God and brought the ark into the temple (1 Kings 5:4-5; 6:2; 7:51—8:11).

5. The Positive Prophets Ministering Faithfully

The positive prophets ministered faithfully, especially concerning Christ in the divine economy. They prophesied a great deal concerning Christ in the divine economy.
