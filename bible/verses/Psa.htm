Introduction to Psalms:
Authors: David wrote seventy-three psalms (3—9; 11—32; 34—41; 51—65; 68—70; 86; 101; 103; 108—110; 122; 124; 131; 133; 138—145); the sons of Korah, twelve (42—49; 84—85; 87—88); Asaph, twelve (50; 73—83); Solomon, two (72; 127); Ethan, one (89); and Moses, one (90). The authors of the other forty-nine psalms are unknown.Time of Writing: Beginning in the eleventh century B.C. (except Psalm 90, written by Moses in the fifteenth century B.C.).Place of Writing: Mainly Jerusalem.

Subject of Psalms:
The Expressions of the Sentiments, Feelings, Impressions,
and Experiences of Godly Men Seeking and Contacting God
through Their Praises, Prayers, and Singing with Exultation
