HYMN NO. 35

11.11.11.11. (Hymns, #660)

In nothing be anxious; the Lord is at hand.
In peace and in vict'ry triumphantly stand;
Let nothing affright thee and nothing dismay,
For Jesus is coming! It may be today.

In nothing be anxious; on Him roll thy care.
Thy sins were laid on Him, thy sorrows He'll bear;
Care free and triumphant press on, still to prove
The strength of His arm and the balm of His love.

In nothing be anxious; should Jesus delay
His strength will be thine till the end of the day.
His arm thou canst lean on; His Spirit will cheer;
Then wherefore be anxious, press on without fear.

 

HYMN NO. 36

8.6.8.6.

Redemption, center of my faith,
    Of hope the cornerstone,
A mystery we scarce explain,
    Yet truth so clearly shown.

Redemption, in my closest thoughts,
    So ever wonderful.
Oh, grace received this sinful one:
    This miracle my jewel.

Yes, many times attacks and pains,
    Temptations, sins flood in,
Thus causing heart and mind to faint.
    And anxious storms begin.

But God's sustaining pow'r of life
    Now helps me contemplate,
To freshly know redemption's work,
    Good news profound and great.

I can declare with surest pledge,
    Though earth be overthrown;
I'll sing redemption's comely song.
    No anxious fears o'erblown.
