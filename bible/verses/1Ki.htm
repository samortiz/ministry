Introduction to 1 & 2 Kings:
Author: Originally, 1 and 2 Kings were one book in the Hebrew Scriptures. Nothing in 1 and 2 Kings indicates who the author was. Some Jewish and Christian teachers have supposed that Jeremiah wrote them.Time of Writing: Some time during the Babylonian captivity of Israel and after 561 B.C. (2 Kings 25:27).Place of Writing: Probably in Jerusalem.Time Period Covered: The two books cover 455 years: 1 Kings covers 119 years, from 1015 to 897 B.C., that is, from the death of David (2:10) to the reign of Ahaziah, the son of Ahab, king of Israel (22:51); 2 Kings covers 336 years, from 896 B.C. to 561 B.C., that is, from the reign of Jehoram, the son of Ahab, king of Israel (3:1-3) to the captivity, during the reign of Evil-merodach in Babylon (25:27).
