CHAPTER FIVE

THE INTRINSIC FACTOR
OF THE WINDS OF TEACHING
FOR THEIR PURPOSE

Scripture Reading: Matt. 13:19; 1 Tim. 1:3-4; 6:3; Eph. 4:14; 2 Tim. 2:18; Acts 8:3; 1 Cor. 1:10-11; Phil. 1:15-18; 3:2

OUTLINE

  1. The intrinsic factor of the winds of teaching:
    1. The winds of teaching:
      1. The winds:
        1. The devilish blowings of the evil one— Matt. 13:19.
        2. Bringing storms into the church.
      2. The blowing teachings:
        1. Different from the New Testament teaching concerning the economy of God for the building up of the unique and organic Body of Christ—1 Tim. 1:3-4; 6:3.
        2. Like the teachings of Judaism, Gnosticism, etc., in the ancient times, and like the teaching of the absolute autonomy of a local church, of no delegated authority, of democracy instead of theocracy, of no spiritual father, etc., in the present time.
    2. The intrinsic factor of such blowing teachings:
      1. The sleight of men—man’s subtle ways of cheating.
      2. That is, the craftiness of men with a view to a system of error—man’s plotted deception to induce people into the satanic system of error— Eph. 4:14.
  2. The purpose of the winds of teaching:
    1. The evil purpose of the enemy Satan versus the eternal economy of God.
    2. To overthrow the faith of some believers—2 Tim. 2:18.
    3. To devastate the church life—Acts 8:3.
    4. To frustrate the building up of the organic Body of Christ.
    5. To tear down the building up of the organic Body of Christ.
    6. To divide the members of the organic Body of Christ—causing endless divisions (sects) in hatred and jealousy, instead of keeping the unique oneness of the Body of Christ in love and kindness—1 Cor. 1:10-11.
