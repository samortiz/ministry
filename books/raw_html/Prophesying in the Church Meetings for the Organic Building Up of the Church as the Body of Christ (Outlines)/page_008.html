OUTLINE SEVEN

PROPHESYING
IN THE CHURCH MEETINGS
FOR THE ORGANIC BUILDING UP
OF THE CHURCH AS THE BODY OF CHRIST

Scripture Reading: Isa. 12:2-6; Lam. 3:22-26; Luke 1:41-45, 46-55, 67-79

  1. The patterns of prophesying:
    1. Isaiah being a prophet—2 Kings 19:2; Matt. 1:22-23; cf. Isa. 7:14:
      1. Most of the book of Isaiah is not foretelling but a speaking forth of the Lord.
      2. Isaiah spoke for the Lord, spoke forth the Lord, and spoke the Lord into the Lord’s children.
      3. Isaiah 12:2-6—a representative portion from Isaiah with the constituting elements of prophesying:
        1. Speaking based upon and with the riches of the Word:
          1. 1) God, Jehovah, is my salvation—Deut. 32:15; Psa. 62:1-2.
          2. 2) Jehovah is my strength and song—Exo. 15:2; Psa. 118:14.
          3. 3) Give thanks unto Jehovah, call upon His name, declare His doings among the peoples—Psa. 105:1; 145:4.
          4. 4) Make mention that His name is exalted— Psa. 34:3; 18:46.
          5. 5) Sing unto Jehovah; for He hath done excellent things: let this be known in all the earth—Exo. 15:1; Psa. 98:1; 8:1.
          6. 6) Cry aloud and shout, thou inhabitant of Zion; for great in the midst of thee is the Holy One of Israel—Psa. 47:1, 5; 48:1-2.
        2. Having the inspiration of the Spirit with a clear view:
          1. 1) New light—drawing water out of the wells of salvation by giving thanks to Jehovah (praising the Lord), calling upon His name, declaring His doings among the peoples, making mention that His name is exalted, singing unto Jehovah, and crying aloud and shouting.
          2. 2) All the prophets in the Old Testament were seers (1 Sam. 9:9), meaning that a prophet, such as Isaiah, by his very constitution, is a person who sees—Isa. 1:1; 2:1.
    2. Jeremiah being a prophet—Jer. 1:1, 4; Matt. 2:17:
      1. Most of the books of Jeremiah and Lamentations are not foretelling but a speaking forth of the Lord.
      2. Jeremiah spoke for the Lord, spoke forth the Lord, and spoke the Lord into the Lord’s children.
      3. Lamentations 3:22-26—a representative portion from Jeremiah’s speaking with the constituting elements of prophesying:
        1. Speaking based upon and with the riches of the Word:
          1. 1) It is of Jehovah’s lovingkindnesses that we are not consumed because His compassions (mercies) fail not—Exo. 34:6-7; Psa. 78:38.
          2. 2) Jehovah’s lovingkindnesses in the morning, His compassions, and His great faithfulness—Psa. 92:1-2; 36:5; 25:6.
          3. 3) Jehovah is my portion, saith my soul; therefore will I hope in Him—Psa. 16:5; 73:26; 33:18.
          4. 4) Jehovah is good unto them that wait for Him, to the soul that seeketh Him—Psa. 27:14, 8; 24:6; 105:4; 119:2.
          5. 5) It is good that a man should hope and quietly wait for the salvation of Jehovah— Psa. 52:9; 40:1; 25:5.
        2. Having the inspiration of the Spirit with a clear view:
          1. 1) New light—Jehovah’s lovingkindnesses and compassions being new every morning and Jehovah being the portion of our soul.
          2. 2) Jeremiah having visions from Jehovah— Lam. 2:9; Jer. 1:11, 13.
    3. Elizabeth’s speaking with the constituting elements of prophesying (Luke 1:41-45):
      1. Based upon the holy Word:
        1. Blessed are you among women—Judg. 5:24.
        2. Blessed is the fruit of your womb—Deut. 28:4.
      2. Under the inspiration of the Holy Spirit, being filled with the Holy Spirit—v. 41.
      3. With a clear view:
        1. Of Mary being blessed among women (v. 42), being the mother of her Lord (v. 43), and being blessed because of believing (v. 45).
        2. Of the Man-Savior:
          1. 1) In His humanity as the fruit of Mary’s womb (Isa. 4:2).
          2. 2) In His deity as her Lord (v. 43: Psa. 110:1).
    4. Mary’s speaking in Luke 1:46-55 with the constituting elements of prophesying:
      1. Speaking with the riches of the Scriptures to praise God her Savior for the blessed fruit of her womb (Luke 1:42-43) as Hannah did for the fruit of her womb that would turn the age— 1 Sam. 2:1-10:
        1. Her soul magnifying the Lord—Psa. 34:2-3; 69:30.
        2. Her spirit having exulted in God her Savior— Hab. 3:18.
        3. God her Savior looking upon the low estate of His slave—1 Sam. 1:11; Psa. 138:6.
        4. All generations counting her blessed—Mal. 3:12.
        5. The Mighty One having done great things for her—Psa. 66:7; 126:2-3; 71:19.
        6. Holy is His name—Psa. 111:9; Isa. 57:15.
        7. His mercy (Exo. 34:6; Psa. 136; Hosea 6:6) is unto generations and generations (Gen. 17:7; Deut. 7:9) to those who fear Him (Psa. 25:14; 111:10; 112:1).
        8. He has done mighty things with His arm— Psa. 89:10; 98:1; Isa. 40:10; 51:9.
        9. He has scattered those who are proud in the understanding of their heart—Dan. 4:37.
        10. He brought down potentates from thrones (Dan. 2:21) and exalted the humble (1 Sam. 2:7; Job 5:11; Psa. 75:7; Prov. 3:34).
        11. He has filled the hungry with good things, and the rich He sent away empty (1 Sam. 2:5; Psa. 107:9).
        12. He has succored Israel His servant (Isa. 41:8-9; 44:21; 49:3) to remember mercy (Psa. 98:3).
        13. Even as He spoke to our fathers, to Abraham and to his seed forever (Gen. 17:19; Psa.132:11).
      2. Mary being full of the knowledge of God’s holy Word:
        1. Qualifying her to be the channel for the Man-Savior’s incarnation.
        2. Indicating that the Man-Savior would grow up in a family that was full of the knowledge and love of God’s holy Word.
        3. An example of sisters being qualified to prophesy—cf. 1 Cor. 11:5; Acts 2:17-18; 21:9.
        4. Showing that we need to be filled with the knowledge of the holy Word in order to prophesy and in order to have Christ, spiritually speaking, conceived in us (cf. John 1:1, 3; Luke 8:11), formed in us (Gal. 4:19), and produced through us (cf. John 15:7-8; Gal. 5:22-23; 2 Pet. 1:4-11).
      3. Being ready in the spirit to receive the inspiration of the Spirit—Luke 1:47.
      4. Having a clear view with the divine enlightenment:
        1. A vision of our spirit for exulting in, contacting, God and our soul for magnifying, expressing, the Lord—vv. 46-47.
        2. A vision of herself—vv. 48-49.
        3. A vision of the people’s poor condition and their need for God’s mercy—vv. 50, 54.
        4. A vision of the divine principle of God filling the hungry with good things and sending away the rich empty—v. 53.
        5. A vision of God’s faithfulness in keeping His word to care for His chosen ones—v. 55.
    5. Zachariah “prophesied” (Luke 1:67) with the constituting elements of prophesying (vv. 68-79):
      1. Full of the Word of God:
        1. Blessed be the Lord, the God of Israel— 1 Kings 1:48; 1 Chron. 29:10; Psa. 41:13; 106:48; Isa. 29:23.
        2. He has visited and accomplished redemption for His people—Exo. 3:16; 4:31; Psa. 111:9.
        3. He has raised a horn of salvation for us in the house of David His servant—1 Sam. 2:10; Psa. 132:17; Jer. 23:5-6.
        4. Quoting God’s speaking through the mouth of His holy prophets in the past—v. 70.
        5. Salvation from our enemies, and out of the hand of all those who hate us—Psa. 106:10.
        6. To show mercy to our fathers, and to remember His holy covenant—Lev. 26:42; Gen. 15:18.
        7. The oath which He swore to Abraham our father—Gen. 22:16-18.
        8. John the Baptizer going before the Lord to prepare His ways—Mal. 3:1; Isa. 40:3.
        9. Christ being the rising sun from on high to visit His people—Mal. 4:2.
        10. Christ appearing to those sitting in darkness and in the shadow of death—Psa. 107:10; Isa. 9:2.
      2. Speaking under the inspiration of the Holy Spirit, being filled with the Holy Spirit (v. 67), to bless God (v. 64; cf. Eph. 1:3).
      3. Speaking with a vision:
        1. Concerning God’s redemptive move for His people unto their salvation—v. 68.
        2. By raising up Christ as a horn of salvation in His humanity (v. 69) and as the rising sun from on high in His deity (v. 78).
        3. A revelation of the Man-Savior’s forerunner (vv. 76-77) and of the Man-Savior in His divine-human Person and His saving work.
