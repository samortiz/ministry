CHAPTER FOUR

THE NEW TESTAMENT PRIESTLY SERVICE

(1)

Scripture Reading: Rev. 1:5-6; Rom. 15:16; 1 Pet. 2:5, 9; Eph. 3:2; 1 Cor. 9:16-17; Eph. 3:8; John 15:16; Psa. 119:147-148; Rom. 8:37; Eph. 5:18b; 2 Tim. 4:2a; John 21:15; 1 Thes. 2:7; Eph. 4:11-12; Heb. 10:24-25; 1 Thes. 2:11

OUTLINE

  1. All the New Testament believers being priests to serve God—Rev. 1:5-6.
  2. The priests of the gospel of God—Rom. 15:16:
    1. Being built up into God's holy priesthood—the whole church becoming the New Testament priesthood of the gospel—1 Pet. 2:5a.
    2. Telling out the virtues (such as love, grace, and forgiveness) of the One who has saved us out of darkness into His marvelous light—1 Pet. 2:9.
    3. Carrying out the stewardship of the grace of God in God's economy—Eph. 3:2; 1 Cor. 9:16-17.
    4. Ministering to others the unsearchable riches of Christ as the grace of God—Eph. 3:8.
    5. Offering up spiritual sacrifices acceptable to God, that is, offering up the saved sinners as members of Christ to constitute the Body of Christ—1 Pet. 2:5; Rom. 15:16b.
    6. Being chosen and appointed by God to bear remaining fruit—John 15:16.
    7. The living of the priests of the gospel of God:
      1. Being revived every morning—Psa. 119:147-148.
      2. Overcoming every day—Rom. 8:37.
      3. Being filled with the Spirit every moment—Eph. 5:18b.
      4. Speaking Christ everywhere—2 Tim. 4:2a.
  3. Feeding the lambs in the Lord's flock—John 21:15:
    1. Through the home meetings in the new believers' homes.
    2. As a nursing mother would cherish her own babes—1 Thes. 2:7.
  4. Perfecting the saints—Eph. 4:11-12:
    1. Through the group meetings:
      1. Not a religious service.
      2. Not a Bible-study meeting.
      3. But the Christians' own assembling—for considering one another, inciting one another to love and good works, and exhorting one another—Heb. 10:24-25.
    2. Bringing the new believers who are under our nourishing to attend the group meetings.
    3. Besides the mutual fellowship, intercession, care, shepherding, and testifying, the main thing in the group meetings being to teach one another that all may be perfected.
    4. Entreating and consoling the believers and testifying to them as a father to his own children—1 Thes. 2:11.
