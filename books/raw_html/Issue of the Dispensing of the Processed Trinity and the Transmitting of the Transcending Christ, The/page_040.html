CHAPTER SIX

THE BODY OF CHRIST

Scripture Reading: Eph. 1:23

OUTLINE

  1. It is the intrinsic significance of the church.
  2. It is the divine constitution of the Triune God with the believers in Christ—Eph. 4:4-6.
  3. It is a mingling of divinity with humanity.
  4. It is an organism, both divine and human, to express Christ.
  5. It is the fullness of the all-inclusive Christ, the One who fills all in all—Eph. 1:23:
    1. Fullness is the issue of the riches of Christ—Eph. 3:8.
    2. Fullness is the expression of the inner riches.
  6. Such an organic Body is:
    1. Undivided and indivisible as Christ is—1 Cor. 1:13a.
    2. Not autonomous.
  7. This unique Body of Christ is expressed in many local churches—Rev. 1:11:
    1. In the divine oneness as it is with the Triune God—John 17:11, 21, 23.
    2. In the divine nature, element, essence, expression, function, and testimony.
    3. Kept by the believers in the practical one accord—Acts 1:14; 2:46; 4:24; 5:12; 15:25; Rom. 15:6.
  8. The divine oneness of the Body of Christ should be kept both in the local churches as the local expressions of the Body and in the universal source and substance.
  9. This genuine oneness of the Body of Christ is one crucial point of the Lord's recovery in the consummating age.
  10. All the problems of the church today are due to the ignorance concerning the Body of Christ.
