CHAPTER FOUR

A VIEW OF GOD’S ECONOMY FROM THE NEW TESTAMENT REVELATION

(2)

Scripture Reading: Eph. 3:2-11; 1:9-11

OUTLINE

  1. The ascended God-man as the Head in His ascension:
    1. Pouring out the Holy Spirit as the ultimate consummation of the Triune God, the realization and reality of Him as the Head, upon all His believers throughout all the ages and among all the nations—Acts 2:1-4, 33.
    2. Baptizing them into one Body, the church—1 Cor. 12:13a.
  2. The church being given to drink of Him as the Spirit and becoming one Spirit with Him—1 Cor. 12:13b; 6:17:
    1. Enjoying His all-inclusive Spirit as the life supply—Phil. 1:19.
    2. Receiving gifts from Him to function as members of His Body—Rom. 12:4-8.
  3. The church as the Body of Christ being expressed as the local churches in all the localities to be the golden lampstands shining forth Christ—Rev. 1:11-12, 20b:
    1. In administrative affairs, being administered locally.
    2. In life, nature, and testimony, being the unique Body of Christ in the whole universe—Eph. 4:3-6; 1 Cor. 12:12.
    3. Having the sevenfold intensified Spirit of Christ for the strengthening of the shining of the golden lampstands—Rev. 1:4; 4:5; 5:6.
  4. The failure of the church:
    1. Becoming desolate and degraded—Rev. 2:14-15, 20.
    2. Being carried away to the mysterious Great Babylon—Rev. 17:5.
  5. The recovery of the church:
    1. Leaving the Great Babylon—Rev. 18:4.
    2. Returning to the original ground of the church, recovering the normal church life—Rev. 3:1, 4, 7-12.
  6. The overcomers in the church and the overcomers among the Old Testament saints eventually constituting:
    1. The bride of Christ—Rev. 19:7-9.
    2. The army of Christ, with Christ defeating Antichrist and his army—Rev. 17:12-14; 19:19-20.
    3. The New Jerusalem in the millennium, as the manifestation of the kingdom of the heavens, reigning with Christ to shepherd the nations—Rev. 3:12; 20:4, 6; 2:26-27.
  7. All the saints in the church and those in the Old Testament ultimately becoming the New Jerusalem in the new heaven and new earth—Rev. 21:1-2,12,14:
    1. As the mutual dwelling place for God and His redeemed in eternity—Rev. 21:3, 22.
    2. As the full expression of God for eternity—Rev. 21:10-11, 23-24.
