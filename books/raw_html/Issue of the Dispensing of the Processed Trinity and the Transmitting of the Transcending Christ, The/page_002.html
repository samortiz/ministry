CHAPTER ONE

THE ISSUE OF GOD THE FATHER'S DISPENSING SPEAKING FORTH
GOD'S ETERNAL PURPOSE

Scripture Reading: Eph. 1:3-6

Hymns: #501, #841

OUTLINE

  1. All the dispensings of the processed Triune God are the spiritual blessings—v. 3:
    1. In the heavenlies.
    2. In Christ.
    3. With which God the Father has blessed the believers, who have been chosen and predestinated by Him.
  2. God the Father's dispensing—vv. 4-6:
    1. In choosing the believers—v. 4:
      1. In Christ.
      2. Before the foundation of the world.
      3. To make the believers holy:
        1. That they may be holy as God:
          1. 1) In His life and in His nature after God's kind.
          2. 2) By regenerating the believers, making them His sons.
          3. 3) The unique way to make the believers, common men, holy like God.
          4. 4) Toward the goal of sonship in His predestination to make men His sons that men may become God.
          5. 5) God's salvation is to bring many sons into His glory, to sanctify men, making them holy like God that they may become God in His nature, life, and expression—Heb. 2:10-11.
        2. Without blemish, without mixture, without foreign particles:
          1. 1) The fallen natural human element.
          2. 2) The flesh.
          3. 3) The worldly things.
        3. Before Him:
          1. 1) To be holy in the eyes of God according to His divine standard.
          2. 2) To be qualified to remain in and enjoy God's presence.
        4. In love:
          1. 1) In the divine love with which God loves us.
          2. 2) In the love which motivates us to love God.
    2. In predestinating the believers—v. 5:
      1. Unto sonship.
      2. Through Christ.
      3. To Himself.
      4. According to the good pleasure of His will.
    3. To the praise of the glory of His grace—v. 6:
      1. To the praise is the issue of the sonship—Rom. 8:19.
      2. Of the glory—of God expressed.
      3. Of His grace—of the enjoyment of God to express God in His glory.
      4. With which He graced the believers:
        1. Putting them into the position to be the object of God's grace.
        2. That they may enjoy all that God is to them.
      5. In the Beloved:
        1. In His beloved Son, who is His delight.
        2. To make the believers His delight in His enjoyable grace.
