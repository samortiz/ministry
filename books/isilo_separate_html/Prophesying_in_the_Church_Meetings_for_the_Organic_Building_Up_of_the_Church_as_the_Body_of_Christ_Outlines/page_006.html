OUTLINE FIVE

PROPHESYING
IN THE CHURCH MEETINGS
FOR THE ORGANIC BUILDING UP
OF THE CHURCH AS THE BODY OF CHRIST

Scripture Reading: Acts 2:42, 46; 5:42; 1 Cor. 14:23a; Matt. 18:20; Eph. 5:18-19; Col. 3:16; Heb.10:24-25; 1 Thes. 5:20; 1 Cor. 14:26, 31, 3-5, 6, 12, 24-25, 29-30, 37, 39.

  1. The God-invented, God-ordained, and God-created way for the church to meet—Acts 2:46; 1 Cor. 14:23a:
    1. Every church meeting should have the full mutuality—Matt. 18:20; Acts 2:42, 46; 1 Cor. 14:23a, 26; Eph. 4:16:
      1. In each attendant having a portion of the riches of Christ to share with others for the organic building up of the Body of Christ—“...whenever you come together, each one has...” (1 Cor 14:26) and “For you can all prophesy one by one” (1 Cor. 14:31).
      2. In speaking—“Speaking to one another in psalms and hymns and spiritual songs” (Eph. 5:19) and “For you can all prophesy one by one...” (1 Cor. 14:31).
      3. In teaching and in admonishing—“Teaching and admonishing one another in psalms, hymns, and spiritual songs”—Col. 3:16.
      4. In considering one another and in exhorting one another—“And let us consider one another for inciting to love and good works, not forsaking the assembling of ourselves together, as the custom with some is, but exhorting one another, and so much the more as you see the day drawing near”—Heb. 10:24-25.
      5. In listening—“Do not despise prophecies”— 1 Thes. 5:20; cf. Rev. 2:7a.
    2. The word spoken in our meetings:
      1. With Christ and the church as the center.
      2. Poems—1 Cor. 14:26; Eph. 5:19; Col. 3:16b:
        1. Psalms—the longest.
        2. Hymns—shorter.
        3. Spiritual songs—the shortest.
        4. For building and for mutual teaching and admonition.
      3. Teachings—1 Cor. 14:26; Acts 5:42:
        1. Concerning the Triune God.
        2. Concerning Christ.
        3. Concerning the Spirit.
        4. Concerning the church.
        5. Concerning life.
        6. Concerning the Christian life.
        7. Concerning other things related to the Christian life.
      4. Revelations—1 Cor. 14:26:
        1. Teachings are the word mainly of our understanding (a word of knowledge—1 Cor. 12:8); revelations are the word mainly of our spirit (a word of wisdom—1 Cor. 12:8).
        2. Concerning the deep things of Christ— 1 Cor. 2:10 and note 3).
      5. Personal testimonies—cf. Acts 15:12.
      6. The gospel for the unsaved and the newly saved—Acts 5:42; 1 Cor. 14:24-25.
      7. Within the limit of the teaching of the apostles—Acts 2:42.
      8. Avoiding anything other than God’s economy— 1 Tim. 1:3-4.
    3. All the attendants in the church meetings can and should speak in prophecy—1 Cor. 14:31, 29-30, 6:
      1. The believers can all prophesy—1 Cor. 14:31— “You can all prophesy one by one....”:
        1. That all may learn.
        2. That all may be encouraged.
      2. The believers should all prophesy—1 Cor. 14:3, 24-25—“If all prophesy...”:
        1. For building up.
        2. For encouragement.
        3. For consolation.
        4. To convict people.
        5. To judge people.
        6. That the secrets of the hearts of the unbelieving and unlearned may be manifested.
        7. That the unbelieving and unlearned may worship God, reporting that God is really among the saints who are meeting together.
  2. Having seen by revelation the God-ordained way of all prophesying in the meetings of the church, the apostle Paul in 1 Corinthians 14 highly recommended it.:
    1. “Earnestly desire the greater gifts”—1 Cor. 12:31.
    2. “Desire earnestly spiritual gifts, but rather that you may prophesy”—v. 1.
    3. “He who prophesies speaks to men building up and encouragement and consolation [lit.]”—v. 3.
    4. “He who prophesies builds up the church”—v. 4.
    5. “I desire that you all...may prophesy: ...greater is he who prophesies...that the church may receive building up”—v. 5.
    6. “Since you are zealous of spirits [lit.], seek that you may excel to the building up of the church”— v. 12.
    7. “If all prophesy and some unbeliever or unlearned person enters, he is convicted by all, he is judged by all; the secrets of his heart become manifest; and so falling on his face, he will worship God, reporting that God is really among you”—vv. 24-25.
    8. “You can all prophesy one by one, that all may learn and all be encouraged”—v. 31.
    9. “Therefore, my brothers, desire earnestly to prophesy”—v. 39a.
  3. In 1 Corinthians 14 Paul also charged all the saints to prophesy in the meetings of the church:
    1. Paul used certain verbs in this chapter to encourage the saints to prophesy:
      1. Pursue—v. 1.
      2. Desire—vv. 1, 39.
      3. Seek—v. 12.
      4. Excel—v. 12.
      5. Learn—v. 31.
    2. Paul, as an example to the Corinthians, aspired to speak words of profit, i.e. to speak to the church...in prophecy—v. 6.
    3. Paul, likewise, encouraged the saints to excel by speaking words of profit for building up, i.e. to come together having a prophecy to speak— vv. 3-5, 12, 26, 31.
    4. Paul promoted all prophesying:
      1. “Now I desire that you all...may prophesy”—v. 5.
      2. “You can all prophesy”—v. 31.
      3. “If all prophesy”—v. 24.
      4. “He is convicted by all”—v. 24.
      5. “He is judged by all”—v. 24.
      6. “Each one has”—v. 26.
    5. Paul equated being a prophet with being spiritual—v. 37.
    6. Paul indicated in this chapter that the saints in Corinth had to be limited in their speaking in tongues and increased in their speaking for the Lord, i.e. prophesying:
      1. Speaking in tongues, even if it is genuine and proper, only edifies the speaker himself.
      2. Prophesying builds up the church.
  4. Principles for prophesying:
    1. Your speaking should be short; do not speak too much:
      1. The more you speak, the dimmer the light may become.
      2. The more you speak, the more the saints may get confused.
    2. You should use the time economically:
      1. To speak a short, clear, profitable word.
      2. To leave time for others to prophesy.
    3. You should practice speaking not longer than three minutes:
      1. If you speak for less than one minute, you should have a main point.
      2. If you speak for more than one minute, you should have a line of development.
    4. You should be systematic:
      1. Do not keep speaking and speaking without a main point or a specific and clear line of development.
      2. Do not go far away from your topic; learn to follow a particular line.
      3. Do not speak in a scattered manner with unrelated points.
    5. You should strike the crucial points clearly.
    6. You should not repeat the points you have already spoken:
      1. Only strike the central points.
      2. Do not explain too much.
    7. You should be confident in what you are speaking.
    8. You should speak with authority:
      1. Having the Word.
      2. Having the Spirit.
    9. You should exercise to have the influencing power; exercise to have the impact that can move others:
      1. This is related to your spirit and your word.
      2. Every sentence you speak must have the proper expression and be uttered with an exercised and released spirit.
    10. You should let the people see the light and get the life supply:
      1. Release the truth.
      2. Dispense the life.
  5. The short prophecies of not more than three minutes are most appropriate for district meetings, for meetings of the whole church, and for small group meetings.
