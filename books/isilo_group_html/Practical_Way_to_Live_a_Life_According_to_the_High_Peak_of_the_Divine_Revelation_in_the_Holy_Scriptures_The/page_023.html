CHAPTER SIX

TO BUILD UP THE BODY OF CHRIST
BY GROWING INTO HIM,
THE HEAD, IN ALL THINGS

Scripture Reading: Eph. 4:15-16

OUTLINE

  1. The Body of Christ is:
    1. The center, reality, and goal of the eternal economy of God, consummating in the New Jerusalem—Rev. 21:1—22:5.
    2. The church:
      1. The congregation of God's called-out ones.
      2. Appearing in many cities to be the local churches as the many local expressions of the one church on the earth, without any division of nationalities, races, and social ranks—Acts 8:1; 13:1; Rev. 1:11.
      3. Although the local churches being the many local expressions, they still remain "the [one] church" (Eph. 1:22-23), "the [unique] church of God" (1 Cor. 10:32; Acts 20:28) universally in its unique unity, unique oneness.
    3. The organism of the processed and consummated Triune God constituted with:
      1. The believers in Christ, the many God-men, as the outward human framework.
      2. The life-giving Spirit, the compound Spirit, as the divine essence.
      3. The all-inclusive Christ, the unlimited Christ, as the divine element.
      4. God the Father, who is over all and through all and in all, as the divine source—Eph. 4:4-6.
    4. The wife of the processed and consummated Christ:
      1. Christ's counterpart, typified by Eve as the counterpart to Adam—Gen. 2:21b-24; Eph. 5:29-33; Rev. 21:2, 9; 22:17a:
        1. Produced out of Christ through His death and resurrection.
        2. Joined back to Christ.
      2. To be one entity with Christ in life and in nature.
    5. The new man—Col. 3:10-11; Eph. 2:15; 4:24:
      1. The corporate God-man:
        1. The first God-man, the firstborn Son of God, being the Head of this corporate God-man—the new man.
        2. The many God-men, the many sons of God, being the Body of this corporate God-man—the new man.
      2. Both the Head and the Body of this new man being in resurrection:
        1. The first God-man, Christ, becoming the Head of the corporate new man in resurrection, in which He became the Firstborn from the dead—Col. 1:18.
        2. The many God-men, the believers in Christ, becoming the Body of the corporate new man in resurrection, in which they, the natural men in race, in religion, in culture, and in social rank, became the regenerated sons of God—1 Pet. 1:3a.
    6. The new creation of God—Gal. 6:15:
      1. The believers in Christ, the corporate new man, the aggregate of the many God-men.
      2. In Christ—2 Cor. 5:17.
      3. In the resurrection of Christ.
      4. Through the death of Christ—Eph. 2:15.
      5. The processed and consummated Triune God consummating a new creation, out of His old creation, with Himself as the creating element.
    7. The household of God—Eph. 2:19b:
      1. The household of the faith—the universal family of God (Gal. 6:10), composed of:
        1. God as the Father.
        2. And the believers in Christ, the many sons of God.
      2. To be the manifestation of God in the flesh—1 Tim. 3:15-16.
    8. The mingling of divinity with humanity:
      1. The processed and consummated Triune God mingled with the regenerated and transformed tripartite men.
      2. God abiding in man and man abiding in God, coinhering and living as one person—John 15:4-5.
      3. The two entities becoming one, with their own respective natures remaining distinct.
      4. Without a third nature produced.
    9. The masterpiece of God—Eph. 2:10:
      1. Created in Christ:
        1. Through His death and resurrection.
        2. With His divine element in its all-inclusiveness.
      2. To walk in the things prepared by God for the accomplishment of His eternal economy.
  2. To build up such a Body of Christ—Eph. 4:12, 16:
    1. By growing in life—Eph. 4:15:
      1. Into Christ, the Head.
      2. In all things.
    2. This growing being the living of a life conformed to the death of Christ by the power of His resurrection and by the bountiful supply of the Spirit of Jesus Christ—Phil. 3:10; 1:19b.
    3. Such a growing affording a corporate building to the Body of Christ—Eph. 4:16:
      1. Joined together through every joint of the rich supply.
      2. And knit together through the operation in the measure of each one part.
