CHAPTER ONE

TO HAVE A CLEAR VIEW
CONCERNING THE ACCOMPLISHMENTS
OF THE PROCESSED AND
CONSUMMATED TRIUNE GOD
(A CRYSTALLIZATION-STUDY OF
THE HOLY SCRIPTURES)

Scripture Reading: Eph. 1:22-23; 4:4-16

OUTLINE

  1. Heavenly, spiritual, mysterious, marvelous, excellent, divine and human as well.
  2. For the organic Body of Christ in the new creation of God.
  3. For the carrying out of the New Testament economy of God.
  4. Incarnation—John 1:14:
    1. To bring God into man by having God incarnated to be a man in the flesh—Gen. 3:15; 22:18; 2 Sam. 7:12-14; Isa. 7:14; 9:6; Micah 5:2.
    2. To mingle divinity with humanity.
    3. To be born of the divine Spirit and of a human virgin—Matt. 1:20.
    4. To produce a God-man—Emmanuel (Matt. 1:23), as the prototype for the mass reproduction of many God-men.
  5. Passing through human living for thirty-three and a half years:
    1. By living a human life to express the divine attributes as human virtues, thus expressing God in humanity.
    2. By living a crucified human life to express the divine life.
    3. To set up a model for the many upcoming God-men—being crucified to live that God might be expressed through humanity.
  6. The all-inclusive death:
    1. Crucifying the flesh of sin—Gal. 5:24; Rom. 8:3b.
    2. Condemning sin (sin is in the flesh [Rom. 7:18] and Christ was made sin by becoming the flesh of sin in its likeness [2 Cor. 5:21a]) and taking away sin (including sins) by shedding the blood —Rom. 8:3b; John 1:29; Heb. 9:26b, 28a; John 19:34b.

      (Note: God as righteousness is embodied in Christ; Satan as sin is embodied in the flesh.)
    3. Destroying the devil, who has the might of death and who is related to the flesh of man—Heb. 2:14; John 12:31b.
    4. Judging the world and casting out its ruler, the devil—John 12:31; Gal. 6:14b.
    5. Crucifying the old man—Rom. 6:6; Gal. 2:20a; 6:14b.
    6. Terminating the old creation by the crucifixion of the old man—Rom. 6:6.
    7. Abolishing the law of the commandments in ordinances—Eph. 2:15a.
    8. Releasing the divine life from the one grain into many grains, signified by the flowing of water at the death of Christ—John 12:24; 19:34b.
  7. The all-conquering resurrection:
    1. Producing the firstborn Son of God by uplifting the humanity of Christ into His divinity and by having Christ born of God (Acts 13:33; Psa. 2:7), that is, by designating the seed of David by the divinity (the Spirit of holiness) of Christ in the power of resurrection to be the firstborn Son of God—Rom. 1:4; 8:29; Heb. 1:3-6.
    2. Regenerating all of God's chosen people to be the many sons of God and the many brothers of the firstborn Son of God—the resurrected God-man, Christ—1 Pet. 1:3; Heb. 2:10; Rom. 8:29.
    3. Consummating the Spirit of God to be the life-giving Spirit—1 Cor. 15:45b:
      1. The Spirit of Christ—the pneumatic Christ, the pneumatized Christ—Rom. 8:9.
      2. The ultimate consummation of the processed and consummated Triune God, who is embodied in the pneumatized Christ realized as the life-giving Spirit.
      3. The reality of resurrection, which is Christ Himself and the processed and consummated Triune God—John 11:25; 1 John 5:6.
  8. The all-transcending ascension:
    1. Christ in His ascension transcending Hades (where the dead people are being held), the earth (where the fallen men are moving against God), the air (where Satan and his power of darkness are acting against God), and all the heavens (where Satan can go)—Eph. 1:20-21; 4:8-10; Heb. 4:14; 7:26.
    2. To be transmitted into the church, which is the Body of Christ, the fullness of the One who fills all in all—Eph. 1:22-23.
    3. To be the Head of the Body, the church, having the first place in all things—Col. 1:18.
    4. To be made:
      1. Both Lord and Christ—Acts 2:36.
      2. Leader (of all the kings) and Savior—Acts 5:31.
    5. To be our High Priest in God's New Testament economy—Heb. 4:14; 7:26; 9:11.
    6. To be the Mediator of the new covenant—Heb. 9:15.
    7. To be the surety of the better covenant—Heb. 7:22.
    8. To be the Minister in the heavenly Holy of Holies—Heb. 8:2.
    9. To be the Paraclete (Advocate) of the New Testament believers—1 John 2:1; John 14:16, 26; 15:26; 16:7.
    10. To be the New Testament believers' Intercessor at the right hand of God and within them as well—Rom. 8:34, 26.
  9. All these excellent, divine, and human accomplishments are for the producing and building up of the organic Body of Christ as the organism of the processed and consummated Triune God, which will consummate in the New Jerusalem in the new heaven and new earth as the expansion and expression of the processed and consummated Triune God in humanity for eternity—Eph. 1:23; 4:12, 16; Rev. 21:1—22:5.
