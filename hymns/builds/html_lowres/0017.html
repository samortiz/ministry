Psa 145:3
1 My Father God, when on Thy vast creation. Gen 1:1
The wonders of the heav'n and earth, I gaze. Isa 42:5
Things great and small, beyond enumeration, Psa 92:5
Which manifest Thy pow'r in untold ways;
Then all my being sings in praise to Thee, Heb 2:12 Psa 149:3 Judg 5:3
How marvelous! How great Thou art! Neh 1:5 Psa 48:1 Psa 145:3
And this I'll sing through all eternity,
How marvelous! How great Thou art!
2 As I enjoy the grace of Thy salvation Titus 2:11 1 Pet 1:10
And contemplate how Thou Thy Son hast sent, John 3:16
Who died that we might be Thy new creation, 2 Cor 5:17
Thy life expressing to the full extent; Matt 5:92 John 17:221
3 When in the church, in blest participation,
I see how millions Thine own life possess,
How they are built to form Thy habitation, Eph 2:22
Containing Thee, Thy fulness to express;
4 As I expect the coming age of fulness
And hope to share the new Jerusalem, Rev 21:2
With all the heavens and the earth in newness Rev 21:1
And all Thou art expressed in all of them;

Judg 5:3 Hear, O kings; give ear, O princes! I will sing; I, to Jehovah. I will sing praise to Jehovah The God of Israel.

Titus 2:11 For the grace of God, bringing salvation to all men, has appeared,
1 Pet 1:10 Concerning this salvation the prophets, who prophesied concerning the grace that was to come unto you, sought and searched diligently,
John 3:16 For God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son, that everyone who believes into Him would not perish, but would have eternal life.
2 Cor 5:17 So then if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation. The old things have passed away; behold, they have become new.
Matt 5:9 Blessed are the peacemakers, for they shall be called the sons of God.
fn 2 Our Father is the God of peace (Rom. 15:33; 16:20), having a peaceful life with a peaceful nature. As those born of Him, if we would be peacemakers, we must walk in His divine life and according to His divine nature. In this way we will express His life and nature and be called the sons of God.
John 17:22 And the glory which You have given Me I have given to them, that they may be one, even as We are one;
fn 1 The glory that the Father gave to the Son is the sonship with the Father's life and divine nature (5:26). The sonship was given so that the Son could express the Father in His fullness (1:18; 14:9; Col. 2:9; Heb. 1:3). The Son has given this glory to His believers that they too may have the sonship with the Father's life and divine nature (v. 2; 2 Pet. 1:4) to express the Father in the Son in the Son's fullness (1:16).
Eph 2:22 In whom you also are being built together into a dwelling place of God in spirit.

Rev 21:1 And I saw a new heaven and a new earth; for the first heaven and the first earth passed away, and the sea is no more.
