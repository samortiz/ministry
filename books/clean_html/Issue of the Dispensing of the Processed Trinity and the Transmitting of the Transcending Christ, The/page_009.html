CHAPTER TWO

THE ISSUE OF GOD THE SON'S DISPENSING SPEAKING FORTH
THE ACCOMPLISHMENT OF
GOD'S ETERNAL PURPOSE

Scripture Reading: Eph. 1:7-12

Hymns: #972, #750

OUTLINE

  1. Through His redemption—v. 7:
    1. The forgiveness of offenses.
    2. Through His blood.
    3. According to the riches of His grace.
  2. To make the believers the inheritance of God—vv. 8-11:
    1. By His abounding grace—vv. 8-10:
      1. In all wisdom and prudence—v. 8:
        1. Wisdom is for God to make a plan and purpose a will in eternity.
        2. Prudence is for God to apply what He has planned and purposed in time.
      2. To make known to all the mystery of His will—v. 9:
        1. According to His good pleasure.
        2. Which He purposed in Himself.
      3. Unto the economy of the fullness of the times—v. 10a:
        1. Economy refers to God's plan for dispensing Himself into His chosen people.
        2. Fullness refers to the completion of all the times for God's dispensing.
        3. Times refers to the ages:
          1. 1) The age of sin.
          2. 2) The age of the law.
          3. 3) The age of grace.
          4. 4) The age of the kingdom.
      4. To head up all things in Christ—v. 10b:
        1. The issue of God's dispensing in all the ages.
        2. Especially through the divine dispensing to the church so that the church is saved from the universal collapse in death and darkness and grows in life to be headed up in Christ in peace and harmony.
    2. In God's designation, making the believers a chosen inheritance of God—v. 11:
      1. According to the purpose of God, who works all things according to the counsel of His will.
      2. Transforming, in the great salvation of Christ, the believers into a treasure (an inheritance of worth) to God in the life element of Christ, to whom they have been redeemed.
      3. That they may inherit God as their inheritance.
  3. To the praise of God's glory—v. 12:
    1. In the believers who have first hoped in Christ.
    2. By the angels and all the positive things in the universe.
    3. Mainly in the millennium and ultimately in the new heaven and new earth.
