CHAPTER THREE

THE ISSUE OF GOD THE SPIRIT'S DISPENSING SPEAKING FORTH
THE APPLICATION OF GOD'S ACCOMPLISHED PURPOSE

Scripture Reading: Eph. 1:13-14

Hymns: #539, #493

OUTLINE

  1. In sealing the believers—v. 13:
    1. As the compound, anointing, life-giving, and indwelling Spirit promised by God.
    2. Through the believers' hearing of the word of the truth, the gospel of their salvation—v. 13a.
    3. Through the believers' believing in Christ.
    4. To saturate the believers continuously unto the redemption of their body—Eph. 4:30.
    5. For transforming the believers into a treasure to God as His inheritance—Eph. 1:11; 2 Cor. 3:18:
      1. By His renewing—Titus 3:5; Rom. 12:2b.
      2. With the life element of the all-inclusive Christ—Phil. 1:19b.
    6. To make the believers a mark of God's image —2 Cor. 3:18b:
      1. With the substance of God.
      2. For the expression of God.
  2. In pledging to the believers—v. 14a:
    1. Through the sealing.
    2. As a foretaste of God.
    3. For a guarantee of God as our inheritance.
    4. Unto the redemption of the body of the believers as God's acquired possession.
  3. To the praise of God's glory—v. 14b:
    1. In the expression of God's image.
    2. In the enjoyment of God's riches.
  4. The dispensing of the Divine Trinity issuing in the Body of Christ as the organic constitution (Eph. 4:4-6) with:
    1. God the Father as the source.
    2. God the Son as the element.
    3. God the Spirit as the essence.
