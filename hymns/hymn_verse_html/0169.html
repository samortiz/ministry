fn 3 The gifts offered to Christ by the magi were gold, frankincense, and myrrh. In figure, gold signifies the divine nature; frankincense, the fragrance of resurrection; and myrrh, the fragrance of death. The offering of these gifts by the magi was undoubtedly under the inspiration of God's Spirit, to indicate the worth of Christ's divine nature and the preciousness of His resurrection and death. In the four Gospels, which are the biographies of Jesus, the things signified by these treasures are seen throughout Jesus' life. Moreover, these precious treasures offered by the magi might have provided for the Lord's journeys from Judea to Egypt and from Egypt to Nazareth.
