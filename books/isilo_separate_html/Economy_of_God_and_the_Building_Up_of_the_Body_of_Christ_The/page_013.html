CHAPTER THREE

A VIEW OF GOD’S ECONOMY FROM THE NEW TESTAMENT REVELATION

(1)

Scripture Reading: Eph. 3:2-11; 1:9-11

OUTLINE

  1. God becoming flesh to bring Himself into man-John 1:14:
    1. Becoming a God-man.
    2. To be experienced by man as grace and reality.
  2. This God-man living a human life on earth:
    1. Living and expressing God in the human life-John 6:57a; Acts 10:38.
    2. Experiencing everything of the human life to be qualified as the Captain of salvation and our High Priest-Heb. 2:10, 17-18.
  3. This God-man, in the human flesh, accomplishing the all-inclusive death on the cross:
    1. A death which redeemed the sinners-Col. 1:20-22.
    2. A death which released the divine life-John 12:24.
    3. A death which produced the Body of Christ-John 12:24.
    4. A death which created the new man-Eph. 2:15-16.
    5. A death which terminated the old creation-Rom. 6:6.
    6. A death which destroyed Satan-Heb. 2:14.
    7. A death which judged the satanic world of darkness-John 12:31.
  4. This God-man entering into resurrection:
    1. Being begotten as the firstborn Son of God, having both divinity and humanity-Acts 13:33.
    2. Regenerating the many sons of God, who constitute the church, the Body of Christ-1 Pet. 1:3; Heb. 2:10, 12.
    3. Becoming the life-giving Spirit-1 Cor. 15:45b.
  5. This God-man, in His resurrection, breathing Himself as the Spirit into His disciples-John 20:22:
    1. To be their life and content-Col. 3:4.
    2. To be with them forever-John 14:20.
    3. To live together with them-John 14:19; Gal. 2:20b.
  6. This God-man, having died and resurrected, ascending to the heavens-Eph. 4:8-12:
    1. Bringing with Him the captives whom He took captive out of the hands of Satan.
    2. Presenting these captives to God.
    3. God giving these captives as gifts to Him, the ascended Head.
    4. He, in the position of His ascension, giving these gifts to the church to build up the Body.
