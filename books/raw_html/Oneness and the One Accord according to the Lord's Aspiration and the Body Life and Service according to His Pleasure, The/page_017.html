CHAPTER FOUR

THE SERVICE OF THE BODY

Scripture Reading: 1 Pet. 2:5, 9; Rev. 1:6; 5:9-10; Rom. 15:16; Eph. 4:11-12; Mark 16:15-16; Luke 10:2-3a, 5-6; 1 Thes. 2:7; John 21:15; 1 Cor. 14:1, 3-5, 12, 23a, 24-25, 31; 1 Pet. 2:2; Rom. 12:1; Col. 1:28-29.

OUTLINE

  1. The service of the Body being the service of the New Testament priests—1 Pet. 2:5, 9; Rev. 1:6; 5:9-10:
    1. Being a built-up priesthood in which everyone participates and coordinates in service.
    2. Their duty being the preaching of the gospel to save sinners, making them sacrifices for the building up of the Body of Christ, the house of God—Rom. 15:16; 1 Pet. 2:9b, 5b; Eph. 4:11-12.
  2. The procedure in the service of the Body:
    1. Preaching the gospel to save sinners—Mark 16:15-16; Luke 10:2-3a, 5-6.
    2. Feeding the lambs as a nursing mother—1 Thes. 2:7; John 21:15.
    3. Perfecting the saints for the building up of the Body of Christ—Eph. 4:11-12.
    4. Prophesying for the Lord for the building up of the church—1 Cor. 14:1, 3-5, 12, 23a, 24-25, 31.
  3. The three steps of offering by the priests of the gospel in the church service:
    1. Preaching the gospel to save sinners, offering them up as sacrifices to God—1 Pet. 2:9b, 5b; Rom. 15:16.
    2. Nourishing the believers so that they may grow up in life to present themselves as sacrifices to God—1 Pet. 2:2; Rom. 12:1.
    3. Laboring and struggling according to the Lord’s operation which operates in us in power to warn and teach every saint in all wisdom, that we may present every one of them full grown in Christ—Col. 1:28-29.
